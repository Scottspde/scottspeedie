About

The lightbulb moment I had when I realised that combining the logical side of my brain, the part that enjoys puzzels and problems with the creative side that enjoys to build and create. Was the moment I realised that programming was for me.

  • February 2014

    An Unexpected Party

    I started as an apprentice in Business Administration within HMRC. At this time I was processing cheques. Through various side jobs I was recognised for my aptitude with IT and statistics. I was moved from cheque processing into the team responsible for reporting the volume and value of all payments collected by HMRC.

  • April 2016

    Out of the Frying-Pan into the Fire

    All the statistical packages used to aggregate the payments and performance were in-house developed. At this time the developer who built them moved on. Having learned much about the structure and code over the previous two years I inherited the maintenance and any requested re-designs.

  • May 2018

    A Short Rest

    I took up a post in HMRC that to this day I find particularly exciting, the team was Payments Strategy. My job was to research the newest payment methods that Fintech's were pushing and apply cost modeling to ensure the best deal for HMRC to modernize their payment channels. This for me was a very exciting toe dip into the world of Banking, Fintechs and Merchants.

  • September 2018

    A Warm Welcome

    December 2017 I'd made the life changing decision to devote myself to learning to code. Therefore, when at this time I was offered a position as a C# developer I couldn't turn it down. I joined a team of two senior developers and two mid-level developers from which I am learning from.
